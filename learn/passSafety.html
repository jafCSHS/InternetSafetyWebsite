How Can I Make a Safe Password?

Password Safety

Password safety is a very important subject in the modern world. Passwords are used for everything from Bank details to Netflix accounts. Since most people do not wish to remember so many different passwords for all their accounts, they tend to use the same few passwords for everything. This is very dangerous.

Keeping Your Information Safe

There are many ways to keep your to keep your information safe just by changing how you use passwords.

  1. Change your password occasionally: Changing your password every once in a while can reduce the risk that your account is compromised. If you change your password every 3 months, then if someone gets your password, they will only have access to your account for 3 months. Although it may not make sense to change all your passwords every three months, it is a good idea to change your password for your imporant accounts every 3 months.
  2. Have a different password for every account: While this can be tedious to remember, it is the safest method. If one account is compromised, the others will be safe. The tediousness can be eliminated by using a password manager, which will allow you to remember 1 very safe password, which is stored only on your computer, and the manager will remember the rest.
  3. Use randomly generated passwords: Randomly generated passwords are much harder to guess than passwords that are based on words. This is because when using words, people tend to use common words, or words that have a personal meaning to them. Randomly generated passwords are much harder to guess, and are much safer. It is also easier to include special characters in randomly generated passwords, which makes them even harder to guess.

Alternate Forms of Authentication

Two-Factor Authentication

One of the most common forms of alternate authentication is two-factor authentication. Two-factor authentication is a method of confirming a user's claimed identity by utilizing a combination of two different components. Two factor authentication is a type of multi-factor authentication. A good example of two-factor authentication is when you log into a website, and after entering your password, you are sent a code to your phone, which you enter to confirm your identity. This is a very secure method of authentication, as it requires both your password and your phone to log in.

Biometrics

Another form of authentication is biometrics. Biometrics is the technical term for body measurements and calculations. It refers to metrics related to human characteristics. Biometrics authentication is used in computer science as a form of identification and access control. This is a very secure method that utilizes a person's unique characteristics, for example, their fingerprint or face, in order to authenticate them.

Passkeys

There is also a new technique that is becoming more readily available: passkeys.

A new login method called passkeys will be introduced in 2023 to address phishing and password reuse problems.

A passkey is some randomly generated data, usually 100-1400 bytes in size, created on your personal device (e.g., phone, laptop, security key) for logging in to a specific website.

Once generated, the passkey is securely stored and associated with the website by your browser, often in your password manager. Instead of entering a password, you can use the passkey to log in to that website. On the login page, you will have the option to "Sign in with a passkey." Selecting this option will prompt your password manager to confirm, and once confirmed, you will be logged in.

Multiple passkeys can be created, with each granting access to a single account on a specific website. If you have multiple accounts on the same website (e.g., personal and business social media accounts), you can use different passkeys for each account.

Each passkey is linked to a domain name, indicating the website it was created for. If someone sends you a link to a deceptive domain name's login page, your browser will not be deceived. Browsers can easily verify an exact match and will not transmit the passkey to a similar-looking domain name, ensuring your security.
