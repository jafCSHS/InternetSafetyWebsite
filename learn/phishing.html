What is Phishing?

Phishing

At first thought, one may think that phishing is the act of catching fish with a rod. But in reality, phishing is a type of cyberattack, used to steal a user's data (for example: credit card information and login details)

But how do they work?

Phishing attacks are carried out by cybercriminals who send malicious emails to a wide range of people. These emails are designed to look like they are from a legitimate source, such as a bank or a trusted company. The email will ask the recipient to click on a link or download an attachment. If they do, they will be taken to a fake website that looks like the real thing. The website will then ask them to enter their personal information, such as their username and password. Once they do, the cyber criminals will have access to their account.

What are the components of a phishing attack?

Phishing attacks are made up of three main components: the email, the website, and the cybercriminal.

  1. The email is the first part of the attack. Pretexting is when the email contains a story or context to make the link more believable. It is designed to look like it is from a legitimate source, such as a bank or a trusted company. The email will ask the recipient to click on a link or download an attachment.
  2. The website is the second part of the attack. The website will look like the real thing, but it will be a fake website designed to steal the user's information. The website will ask them to enter their personal information, such as their username and password. Once they do, the cyber criminals will have access to their account.
  3. The cybercriminal is the third part of the attack. They are the ones who send the email and create the fake website. They are also the ones who will have access to the user's account once they enter their information.

How can I avoid phishing attacks?

There are a few ways to avoid phishing attacks. First, you should always be wary of emails that ask you to click on a link or download an attachment. If you are not sure if an email is legitimate, you should contact the company directly to verify that it is real. You should also make sure that you have a strong password for all of your accounts. This will make it harder for cyber criminals to access your accounts. To learn more about suspicious links, check out our article on Suspicious Links.

Never give out your personal information online without checking first. Ensure that whereever you are putting your information is who they say they are and that they are a trusted source. If you are unsure, do not give out your information and contact the company through official means.
